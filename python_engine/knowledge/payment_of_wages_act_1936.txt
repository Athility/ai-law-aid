Chapter I: Responsibility for Payment of Wages
Under Section 3 of the Payment of Wages Act, 1936, the employer is personally responsible for the payment of all wages required to be paid to persons employed by them. This includes contractors and sub-contractors, but ultimate responsibility remains with the principal employer if the contractor defaults.

Chapter II: Time of Payment of Wages
Section 5 mandates that wages must be paid before the 7th day of the following month for establishments with less than 1000 employees. For establishments with 1000 or more employees, wages must be paid before the 10th day. Holding back salaries or delaying salaries without mutual consent is a violation of this act.

Chapter III: Unauthorized Deductions
Section 7 prevents employers from making unauthorized deductions from an employee's salary. Only specified deductions (like income tax, provident fund, court-ordered attachments) are legally valid. Deducting pay arbitrarily as a "fine" or "penalty for underperformance" without due process is illegal.

Chapter IV: Recovery of Unpaid Wages
Section 15 allows the employee to file an application before the Authority appointed under the Act to recover delayed or unpaid wages, alongside compensation. The compensation can be up to 10 times the amount of the deduction made or Rs 3000 for delayed wages.

Where to Approach
Aggrieved employees whose salary has not been paid can approach the Labour Commissioner's office, file a claim under Section 33C(2) of the Industrial Disputes Act, or send a legal notice demanding dues within 15 days, failing which a civil suit for recovery or an insolvency petition under IBC (if a corporate employer) can be initiated.
