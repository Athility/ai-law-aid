Chapter I: Hacking and Data Theft
Section 66 of the Information Technology Act, 2000 penalizes hacking with a computer system, data theft, and virus attacks. Any person who fraudulently or dishonestly damages a computer system, downloads data without permission, or causes denial of access faces imprisonment up to three years or a fine up to five lakh rupees.

Chapter II: Identity Theft and Cheating
Section 66C punishes Identity Theft. Whoever fraudulently or dishonestly makes use of the electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature of any other person faces imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and a fine up to Rupees one lakh.
Section 66D covers Cheating by Personation by using computer resources. This applies when someone creates a fake social media profile or impersonates another person online to cheat victims.

Chapter III: Privacy Violations
Section 66E deals with the violation of privacy. Publishing or transmitting images of private areas of any person without their consent is a punishable offense. This is commonly used in cases of revenge porn or hidden cameras. 

Chapter IV: Cyber Harassment and Obscenity
Section 67 penalizes publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form. Subsequent offenses carry higher penalties of up to seven years' imprisonment. 

Where to approach:
Victims of cybercrime should immediately report incidents on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the toll-free helpline number 1930. Complaints can also be registered at the nearest Cyber Cell or local police station.
