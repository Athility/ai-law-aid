Chapter I: Registration of Real Estate Projects
Under Section 3 of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA), a promoter cannot advertise, market, book, sell or offer for sale any plot, apartment, or building without first registering the real estate project with the RERA authority.

Chapter II: Return of Amount and Compensation
Section 18 is the most critical right for homebuyers. If the builder or promoter fails to complete or is unable to give possession of an apartment, plot, or building by the date specified in the agreement for sale, the homebuyer has two options:
1) Withdraw from the project: The promoter is legally obligated to return the entire amount received from the buyer, along with interest at the prescribed rate.
2) Stay in the project: If the allottee does not intend to withdraw, the promoter must pay interest for every month of delay, till the handing over of the possession.

Chapter III: Alteration in Plans
Section 14 mandates that the promoter cannot make any additions or alterations to the sanctioned plans, layout plans, and specifications in respect of the homebuyer's apartment without the previous written consent of the buyer.

Chapter IV: Filing Complaints
Section 31 allows any aggrieved person (including an allottee/homebuyer) to file a complaint with the RERA Authority or the adjudicating officer for any violation or contravention of the Act by a promoter, buyer, or real estate agent. 

Where to approach:
Complaints should be filed through the respective State RERA's online portal using the standard forms. Homebuyers do not strictly need lawyers to represent them in RERA tribunals, making it consumer-friendly.
