Chapter I: Definitions
Section 3 defines "Domestic Violence" broadly to include actual abuse or the threat of abuse, whether physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, or economic. Harassment by way of unlawful dowry demands to the woman or her relatives is explicitly covered under this definition. Economic abuse includes deprivation of financial resources to which the aggrieved person is entitled under law or custom.

Chapter II: Right to Reside in a Shared Household
Section 17 states that every woman in a domestic relationship shall have the right to reside in the shared household, whether or not she has any right, title or beneficial interest in the same. She cannot be evicted or excluded from the shared household by the respondent except in accordance with the procedure established by law.

Chapter III: Protection and Residence Orders
Section 18 empowers the Magistrate to pass a Protection Order prohibiting the respondent from committing any act of domestic violence, aiding or abetting acts of violence, or attempting to communicate with the aggrieved person without permission.
Section 19 allows the Magistrate to pass a Residence Order restraining the respondent from dispossessing the aggrieved person or disturbing her possession from the shared household.

Chapter IV: Monetary Relief and Custody
Section 20 provides for Monetary Relief where the Magistrate can direct the respondent to pay adequate, fair, reasonable, and consistent relief to meet the expenses incurred and losses suffered by the aggrieved woman and her children.
Section 21 allows temporary Custody Orders of any child or children to the aggrieved person to prevent the respondent from forcibly taking them away.

Where to Approach
Victims should approach the local Protection Officer (PO), any police station, an NGO, or directly file a petition before the Magistrate court under Section 12 of the PWDVA. Free legal aid is available via DLSA.
